LEVEL 06: RISKS

6. ETF Risks (Yes, They Exist)

1. Market Risk

There is no protection. If Nifty falls 50%, your ETF falls 50%. You are fully exposed to the market.

2. Liquidity Risk

Some niche ETFs have low volume. If you want to sell 10,000 units, there might be no buyers, forcing you to sell at a loss (Slippage).

3. Tracking Error

The ETF might return 9.8% when Nifty gave 10%. The difference is lost to friction, cash holdings, and expenses.

LEVEL 08: THE SWITCH

8. ETF vs Mutual Fund (Indian Reality)

Mutual Funds

Good For: Beginners who want SIP automation and don't want to open a Demat account.

Cons: High cost, NAV transparency only at day end.

ETFs

Good For: Investors who want control, lower costs, and can place buy orders themselves.

Pros: Real-time price, ultra-low cost.

Trend: Advanced investors often migrate from MFs to ETFs once their capital grows to save on commission.

LEVEL 10: TIMING

10. SIP vs Lump Sum in ETFs

Strategy: The Hybrid Approach

SIP (Manual): Buy ETFs consistently on the 1st of every month regardless of price. This averages your cost.

Lump Sum (Sniper): Keep cash ready. When the market falls 5-10%, deploy bulk cash into your Core ETFs.

Note: Most brokers now offer "SIP in ETF" features automatically.
