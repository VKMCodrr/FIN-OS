LEVEL 05: THE MENU

5. Types of Instruments (Indian Context)

1. G-Secs (Sovereign)

Issued by Govt of India. Risk: Zero. Returns: Moderate. The safest place for money.

2. Corporate Bonds

Issued by companies (Reliance, Tata). Risk: Low to High. Returns: Higher than Govt.

3. Fixed Deposits (FD)

Issued by Banks. Safe but tax-inefficient. Comfort food, not superfood.

4. NCDs / Debentures

High interest corporate debt. Watch out for credit rating.

LEVEL 11: MYTH BUSTING

11. How Indians Misunderstand Debt

"Debt is Risk Free"

FALSE

Companies default. Rates change. Debt funds can give negative returns in short term.

"FD is Always Safe"

FALSE

FDs are safe from market loss, but safe from Inflation? No. You lose purchasing power.

"Higher Interest = Better"

FALSE

High interest usually means the company is desperate for money. High risk of default.

LEVEL 12: THE SILENT THIEF

12. The Inflation Reality Check

If your Debt Return < Inflation, you are becoming poorer.

FD Return 6.0%
Inflation 7.0%
Real Return -1.0%

Debt protects capital value, but not always purchasing power. That's why you need Equity.
