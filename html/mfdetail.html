LEVEL 05: THE MENU

5. Types of Mutual Funds (Indian Context)

There are 2,500+ schemes. You only need to understand these 4 categories.

A. Equity Funds (Stocks)

High Risk, High Return. For goals > 5 Years.

  • Large Cap: Top 100 Companies (Stable).
  • Mid Cap: Next 150 Companies (Fast Growth).
  • Small Cap: Emerging Companies (Volatile).
  • ELSS: Tax Saving (3 Year Lock-in).

B. Debt Funds (Loans)

Low Risk, Low Return. For goals < 3 Years.

  • Liquid Funds: Like a Savings Account (Safe).
  • Corporate Bond: Lending to companies.
  • Gilt Funds: Lending to Govt of India (Safest).

C. Hybrid Funds (Mix)

Balanced Risk. Best for conservative investors.

  • Aggressive Hybrid: 70% Equity, 30% Debt.
  • Balanced Advantage: Auto-adjusts based on market.

    9. Risk & Indian Mistakes

    Overlapping

    Buying 4 different Large Cap funds. You own HDFC Bank 4 times. You are paying 4 fees for the same portfolio.

    Churning

    Stopping SIP because the market fell 10%. This is exactly when you should be buying.

    Sector Trap

    Buying "IT Fund" because IT was up 50% last year. This is "Return Chasing". Usually ends in loss.
